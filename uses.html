How is Artificial Intelligence Used in Healthcare?

AI is being used in so many ways now. There has been a surge in developments whether it's key infrastructure such as IT systems or medical machinery and the software associated, the world of AI in healthcare is upon us and it is here to stay.

Below is a showcase of all the incredible ways AI is being used in today's healthcare.

Radiology & Dermatology

Diagnostic Image Analysis

DERM is an AI skin lesion analysis technology primarily used for screening, triage and assessment of possible skin cancer lesions in people aged 18 or over.

Possible Benefits

  • Faster Initial Screening
  • Early Detection of Life-Threatening Conditions
  • Reduced Workload For NHS CLinicians

Possible Negatives

  • False Positives or Negatives
  • Limited Transparency in How Decisions Are Made
  • Over-reliance on Automated Decisions

Consider: Would you feel comfortable if this AI system was used as an intial screening tool, providing a doctor would look over the results?

Administrative Operations

Triaging Patients & NHS App Developments

New AI features in the NHS App: My Companion. AI-powered tools aimed at helping patients manage appointments, ask better queries regarding their health, and receive trusted information.

Often referred to as My Companion, which is designed to support patients administrative interactions when using the NHS services, bridging gaps in information, enabling patients to articulate concerns more effectively during their consultation.

Possible Benefits

  • Enhanced Patient Support & Experience
  • Reduces Number of Missed Appointments
  • Improved Administrative Efficiency

Possible Negatives

  • Not suitable for those without digital access or literacy
  • Risk of Misinterpretation of AI-generated guidance, particularly for patients with serious or multiple conditions.
  • Over-reliance on Automated Decisions

Consider: Would you feel content with using this AI system before asking for a GP appointment?

Diagnosis Support - Oncology

Treatment Planning & Support

AI systems in this instance are used to support clinicians in planning an managing treatment, in more complex areas such as cancer and mental health.

Tailored support depending on the patients condition using Clinical Decision Support Systems, providing analysis of patient history, test results and clinical guidelines to then suggest possible treatment options

Possible Benefits

  • Supports Complex Decision-Making
  • Assists High-workload Staff with ready to go treatment options
  • Potential for More Personalised Care

Possible Negatives

  • Lack of Patient Choice
  • Risk of Bias in Underlying Data
  • Over-reliance on Automated Decisions

Consider: How would you feel knowing that an AI system helped tailor your treatment options, especially for such a serious health condition?
